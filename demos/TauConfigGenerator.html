System Identity
Give your symbolic system a name. This becomes the exported constant name in the generated TypeScript file (e.g. ALCHEMY_CONFIG).
Archetype Count
How many archetypes does your system define? 1–9 are natural digital roots — they always exist. Numbers 10+ become typal numbers: multi-digit values that halt digital-root reduction. The alphabet will cycle over your full root set (e.g. 12 archetypes = A-L, M-X, Y-Z). Max 26 (one per letter).
9 roots · no typal numbers
Archetype Definitions
For each digital root, define the archetype name, a description, a resonance keyword (one word, uppercase by convention), a resonance description, and a scene function (how this archetype behaves in narrative structure).

Roots 10+ show an editable typal-number field — you can reassign which number maps to that slot if your system uses non-sequential typal values (e.g. roots 1–9 plus 12 and 24).
